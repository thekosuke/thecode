Background

I’m currently designing the Family Operating System® to keep families organized and prepared in one place.

As a senior designer, I’m responsible of improving all facets of the product—including the web app, iOS app, and managing the design system. Trustworthy requires system-level problem solving since many features and components live in different surfaces. Below are the product and features I designed and (some) shipped during my time here.

Partner Portal: New Platform

The Partner Portal is Trustworthy's B2B2C initiative in which third-party partners, such as wealth advisors, are invited to use Trusworthy to serve their clients' needs. I delivered the MVP design in one week to help the business development team share a usable proof of concept and unlock users in bulk.

Trustworthy's new B2B2C initiative

Contacts: New Product

Trusted professionals already existed within Trustworthy as a way to link them to relevant assets, but there was no place for customers to save and manage these individuals. Therefore, I designed Contacts as a new item in the main navigation, where customers can visit to view and manage trust individuals outside of their family.

Contacts page

'Add contact' modal

List View: New Feature

The default UI displayed items in a 3 column grid. This approach made it difficult for users to quickly find relevant items when they have many assets in a single category. To allow users to see a concise list of their assets, I designed a list view that users can toggle and save as their preferred view.

List view for each categories

Dashboard: UX/UI Improvement

The previous Dashboard hid important information such as reminders and collaborators below the viewport, so users were unable to get a glimpse of what they need to worry about upon login. To address this issue, I introduced a third column layout in the Dashboard to make key information accessible.

Before and after of the Dashboard

Actions & Reminders: UX/UI Improvement

Actions and Reminders previously existed inside a popup window that were difficult to see and navigate. Thus I turned them into side drawers that enable split view that doesn't get in the way of other content.

Before and after of Reminders

Before and after of Recommended assets

Modals: UI Improvement

The previous modals contained numerous problems such as information hierarchy and its inability to present content concisely. Users often didn't discover certain information or buttons since they were required to scroll down with the modal. This is a UI improvement to clean up all the modals and communicate each moment clearly.
