Background

Otter.ai is a speech to text transcription service based on artificial intelligence and machine learning. On August 2022, I worked with Otter as a contractor for one month to solve product design problems and act as a consultant to their design team.

One of the projects I worked on was to redesign their icon library to align better with their brand by developing a cohesive design language. Otter’s existing icon library had not been updated since its conception, and contained various flaws from a aesthetic and functional standpoint.

Problem

The past design contained inconsistencies in size and border width, which created technical difficulties when implementing the icons in the UI. For instance, each icon appeared to be in different proportions when they are all in 24px.

Even though the icons are in outline style, they lacked cohesion due to the lack of distinct characteristics that should exist among all the icons. From a functional standpoint, the icons were either too thin and hard to see when placed on a dark background, had inconsistent proportions that appeared like they're in different sizes, or inconsistent specifications in the Figma file that required custom adjustments.

Design Principles

After studying the existing icons and understanding its problems, my goal was to establish a set of principles that will allow designers in the future to add on to this new library. The existing icons were simple, modern, friendly, and sometimes quirky. Each icon was reduced to its minimal form, expressing essential characteristics. I aimed to adopt these principles while incorporating the functional aspect to it.

While adopting the current design language, the new icons must respect practicality in which considers legibility and implementation. Given the existing brand and problems, the goal was to design a icon library that is unique to Otter by being practical, intuitive, and whimsical.

Final Design

The visual concep was to Substract and Underline (figuratively) — as a general room of thumb, remove an element from the conventional icon to achieve a minimal effect while creating a visual element of recognition.

For example, the ‘Delete’ icon replaces the vertical lines inside the trash can with a circle that is reminiscient of the Otter ‘O’. If an icon has a smaller icon in the corner, the smaller icon should be filled to maximize legibility and add contrast to the outline form.

Redesigned Icon Library

The icons were created in a 24px by 24px grid with 2px padding to create a consistent breathing space for each icon. I set the stroke weight to 1.75px to create icons that can easily be seen on a dark background, but also to appear round and approachable in a small form. The round caps and joints supported this notion of a friendly design language. All icons were converted into a flat vector that scales proportionately when set to different sizes.
