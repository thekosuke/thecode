italiaDesign
Overview

In 2018, I was one of twelve senior students selected to participate in italiaDesign, an 8-month undergraduate field school program offered by Simon Fraser University. The purpose of field school is to be exposed to a rich design culture and understand the discipline of design in a foreign environment. The team conducted in-field research and interviews which were produced into a series of films, which we capsuled in a website that we created after our experience in Italy.

Challenge

Develop an brand identity that is unique to the team and translate this voice onto a web experience that creatively captures our learnings from the past 8-months.

Insight

Past field school sites each carried their own unique identity, though the architecture of these sites have created a template-like pattern that overlooked the intention behind the organization of the content.

Approach

Past field school sites each carried their own unique identity, though the architecture of these sites have created a template-like pattern that overlooked the intention behind the organization of the content.

We established key principles to guide our decision making: Embrace Ambiguity, Respect the Copy, and Hero the Designers. We wanted the experience to reflect these values to communicate where we were coming from.

Craft a web experience that challenge expectations for those who have visited past field school sites by rethinking the template.

Execution

We created a landing page that encourage people to scroll and engage with the copy to tell our intention with the site upfront. By packaging the films with a specific purpose, we implied a linear narrative and gave meaning to the organization of content.
