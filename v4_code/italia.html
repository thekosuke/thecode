italiaDesign

Overview

In 2018, I was one of twelve senior students selected to participate in italiaDesign, an 8-month undergraduate field school program directed by Russell Taylor. The team interviewed and filmed emerging and established designers in Italy to learn how design is understood in a foreign context. We then capsuled these films in a web experience to contribute to the legacy of italiaDesign field school.

Challenge

We challenged ourselves to develop a brand identity that is unique to our experience in Italy. We wanted to translate this identity onto a web experience that creatively captured our learnings from field school in order to communicate the purpose of our study.

Insight

Past field school sites each carried their own unique identity, though the annual launch of these sites have created a template-like pattern that lost a sense of purpose behind the organization of the content. We challenged ourselves to go beyond the template by rethinking our process.

Approach

The initial process was to establish our visual identity before considering the user experience, though this lead to a linear process that overlooked the actual content.

We decided to follow a parallel process in which we begin considering the organization of content along with the user experience to enhance our brand. This was done by simultaneously designing and building to actually see how our strategy is being reflected in the experience.

We established design principles to guide our decision making: Embrace Ambiguity, Respect the Copy, and Highlight the Designers. We wanted the experience to reflect these values to communicate where we were coming from.

Ambiguity was an excerpt from The Vignelli Canon, in which spoke about how making contradictions in your design can enhance the quality of work. We wondererd how this philosophy could be applied to a web experience.

While iterating on the landing page, I had aimed to contain all the information in one view to allow immediate access to all the content. Though I realized this was simply a false constraint that limited our opportunity to challenge people’s expectations.

It’s important to reduce frictions to achieve an intuitive user experience, but I also value the emotional impact that can be evoked through interactions. We wanted to challenge expectations with interactions that helped tell the greater narrative that we strategically constructed.

Execution

We created a landing page that encourage people to scroll and engage with the copy to tell our intention with the site upfront. This first impression left a sense of ambiguity that kept the audience curious enough to dig deeper.

By packaging the films into three distinct volumes, we implied a linear narrative and gave meaning to the organization of content. With the hover interaction, we were able to showcase the powerful designer portraits along with the film title and volume description.

We wanted the films to be the first piece of content people engage with since it told the designer's story while allowing us to highlight key moments of the interview.

Because our films focused on the designers' unique practice rather than their work, we saw value in including the work along with bite-size quotes as extended content for people to actually see how these designers apply their design philosophy onto their work.

With the slideshow format, we were able to imply a linear narrative through the work while presenting relevant content piece-by-piece.

With the left aligned typography and the use of full bleed portraits, the site was able to translate seamlessly on mobile.
