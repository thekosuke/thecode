An Inclusive Symbol for the Local Community

Breast Cancer Hawaii

|

Logo Design

Guidelines

|

2022

In the summer of 2021, I volunteered as a design advisor for Breast Cancer Hawaii. The organization's mission is to enhance the quality of life for Hawaii's breast cancer community by facilitating connections with relevant resources and providing meaningful support. Throughout my tenure, I contributed by designing logos for various programs and creating guidelines that enable others to apply the brand consistently.

This is the logo design process I undertook for one of Breast Cancer Hawaii's programs, named Hearts for Ohana.

Core Values

Breast Cancer Hawaii's brand is defined by core values that distinguish the organization, underscoring its uniqueness, value, and success.

To initiate the brand enhancement, I engaged in discussions with the founder to better understand and articulate the values and experiences the organization aims to offer its participants. Through our dialogues, it became clear that the visual language needed to be inclusive of everyone navigating a breast cancer diagnosis, with a particular focus on providing upliftment to those feeling overwhelmed.

It was crucial to convey that breast cancer affects all genders and has implications for the entire family. Specifically, Hearts for Ohana is designed to support family units, especially children, recognizing a significant service gap in Hawaii for young family members of parents facing a cancer diagnosis.

Exploration

My objective was to encapsulate Breast Cancer Hawaii's core values through various design elements within the logo. In this endeavor, I aimed for a contemporary aesthetic that communicates professionalism and trustworthiness, yet still retains an element of playfulness.

Logomark

In Hawaii, 'Ohana' signifies 'family'â€”a concept that extends its meaning to 'flower' in Japanese. Given the founder's Japanese heritage and the prominence of Japanese language and culture in Hawaii, I delved into integrating these dual meanings with the brand's core values. The result is the Hearts for Ohana logomark, a creative interpretation of this double entendre that aligns with the program's aims and Breast Cancer Hawaii's visual identity.

I designed the logomark to culturally resonate with the local community, embodying Breast Cancer Hawaii's core values and the program's dedication to supporting families. The symbol merges two hearts with a monstera leaf, a plant prevalent in Hawaii, to form its central motif. This abstraction not only serves as the focal point but also connects the hearts, with its edges reaching out to bridge the two elements. The color scheme was chosen to promote cancer awareness as a gender-neutral issue.

Addressing the need for accessible fonts in the brand guidelines, I selected Quicksand as the primary typeface for its approachable yet clear appearance, lending a warm tone to the brand. Georgia, with its serif elegance, serves as a complementary contrast, enhancing the playful yet sophisticated visual language.
