A Digital Handbook for a Design Conference

Touchpoint Design Conference

|

Art Direction, Web Design/Development

|

2019

Website: sfu.ca/idc/2019

The Touchpoint Interaction Design Conference is part of Simon Fraser University and the School of Interactive Arts and Technology. Touchpoint is run annually, with each year featuring its own visual identity for their website. I volunteered to define the identity and build the website for Touchpoint 2019.

Concept

In forming my vision for the 2019 site, I started by reviewing past Touchpoint websites. Observing recurring themes and constraints, I aimed to infuse the site with a playful identity that would draw from historical inspirations.

The design direction found its roots in the Bauhaus movement, an iconic force in modern design emphasizing the relationship of art to society and technology, and advocating for design's accessibility to everyone.

I delved into key visual characteristics of Bauhaus graphic design, leveraging them to refine the concept and digitally embody the Bauhaus ethos of foundational exploration.

Visual references that supported the overall design direction

My pursuit of a visual identity sought to capture the essence of the playfulness I envisioned. This journey led me to a form that was both recognizable and enigmatic, open to a wealth of interpretations. A distinctive motion sequence further defined the visual language, laying the groundwork for the site's identity.

The symbol for Touchpoint 2019

Logotype animation

In choosing a typeface, Avenir Next emerged as a fitting tribute to my objective of modernizing a historical concept. Avenir stands as a marker of typographic evolution, embodying Adrian Frutiger's forward-looking approach and aspiration to blend past aesthetics with future possibilities.

This resonance with Frutiger's vision, bridging history with contemporary influences, aligned perfectly with my inspiration drawn from both the past and present.

Typography and color selection

Mobile-First

I designed and developed with a mobile-first approach, anticipating that visitors would primarily access the site on their phones to view guest speakers during the conference. My goal was for the site to serve as a digital handbook for conference attendees.

The driving concept for the user experience was 'Function & Play' â€” to create a web experience that prioritizes functionality while incorporating playful elements to engage the audience. Recognizing the need to include guest bios for the 2019 site, I explored various presentation methods to optimize content display without overwhelming the mobile screen.

Switching to dark mode

Ergonomics guided the placement of the navigation bar at the bottom of the phone screen, ensuring easy access to the menu when the phone is held with one hand. I implemented a dark mode, predicting its suitability for the dimly lit conference environment. This feature not only catered to user comfort but also introduced a new color scheme, offering a delightful surprise to the audience.

Landing Sequence

The first impression sets the tone for the rest of the experience, whether that's meeting someone or entering a building. I believe this applies to visiting a website as well. On landing, we wanted to take a moment to wow the audience while reinforcing our concept of Play.
